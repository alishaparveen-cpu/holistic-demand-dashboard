# GMB Playbook — STI Specific Overlay

**Read this AFTER `PLAYBOOK_NEW_CLINIC.md`.** This is the STI-specific layer on top of the general new-clinic playbook — additional items to do, not a replacement.

## Crucial framing — what GMB can and cannot do for STI

Per the 4-round audit completed before this (`PATTERN_REPORT.md`):

| What GMB **can** do | What GMB **cannot** do |
|---|---|
| Get STI-search-intent users to find the clinic | Make a clinic convert that traffic to STI consults |
| Surface "STD/STI testing" as a recognized service | Fix EMR diagnosis tagging discipline |
| Reach 4–5% STI-keyword impression share (network normal) | Move that beyond ~5% (search demand ceiling) |
| Maintain trust signals via review velocity + ratings | Substitute for in-clinic intake protocol |

**Bottom line:** Top-STI clinics (Velachery 39%, Mogappair 35%) and bottom-STI clinics (Ashok Nagar 2%, Arekere 4%) have **near-identical GMB profiles and near-identical STI-search-intent reaching them**. The conversion gap is at the consultation/tagging step inside the clinic, not at the GMB step. Set realistic expectations: a perfectly-executed STI GMB playbook gets the clinic to the network's STI-impression baseline. It does NOT guarantee the clinic will book STI consults at top-tier rates.

That said — **execute this playbook anyway**, because:
1. STI hygiene is the floor (without it, you're invisible to condition-specific search)
2. The network's 31-service STI template is genuinely above-average for healthcare GBPs in India
3. It compounds with the EMR-tagging fix when that lands

## What gets layered on top of the general playbook

### 1. Title — include the STI mention exactly once

Standard template: `Allo Health, [Hood] - Best Sexologist in [City] | Sex Doctor | Sex Therapist | Sex Clinic | STD/STI Testing`

**"STD/STI Testing" at the end is the only STI mention.** Do not add "best STD clinic" or "HIV clinic near me" or "syphilis testing" — Google flags this as keyword stuffing and either suspends or down-ranks. The prior audit confirmed top-STI and bottom-STI clinics use identical title hygiene; the title is not where the variance comes from.

**Wrong-city titles are real and damaging.** The prior audit caught Tatya Tope Nagar Nagpur titled as "Nashik" (wrong city entirely) and Saraswathipuram Mysuru titled as "Mysore" (split search demand). For STI specifically: pin the city spelling Google uses (locality field on the location) and use it verbatim.

### 2. Categories — STI-relevant ones, in order

| Slot | Category | Mandatory for STI |
|---|---|---|
| Primary | Sexologist | Yes |
| Additional 1 | STD clinic | **Yes** |
| Additional 2 | STD testing service | **Yes** |
| Additional 3 | HIV testing center | **Yes** |
| Additional 4 | Sex therapist | Yes (general) |
| Additional 5 | Sexual health clinic | Yes (general) |

Categories 2–4 are STI-specific. Without them, the profile doesn't show up for "STD clinic near me", "HIV testing center", etc.

### 4. Photos — STI-relevant content (within the 30+ total)

Of the 30 photos the general playbook requires, allocate ~4–6 to STI-specific signals:

- [ ] **Test kit / sample collection setup** (1 photo) — visual proof that testing happens here
- [ ] **Lab partnership signage or test report** (1) — shows the chain to results
- [ ] **"Confidential consult" room sign or door** (1) — privacy signal
- [ ] **Doctor's medical degree certificates** (1–2) — credentialing matters for STI-anxious patients more than for general consults
- [ ] **Sample test menu / pricing displayed at reception** (optional) — helps with the "fees high" complaint we see across all cities; transparency builds trust

DO NOT include images that show patient faces, conditions, or anything that violates Google's healthcare content policy. STI imagery especially trips automated content filtering — keep visuals abstract / facility-focused.

### 5. Posts — STI cadence

Within the general posting rhythm (1–2/week), allocate roughly **1 post per month to STI content**. Don't overdo it — the prior audit found Allo's STI keyword impression share is 4.6–5.0% network-wide regardless of content effort; that's the search ceiling. Excessive STI content also makes the profile look one-note to general sex-health searchers.

**Post types that work:**
- "What is HIV PEP and when do you need it" (information)
- "Confidential STD testing process — what to expect" (anxiety reduction)
- "STI window periods explained" (timing/clinical)
- "Treatment for Chlamydia / Gonorrhea — what the doctor will recommend" (treatment-clarity)

Avoid: scare-tactic posts, posts that name specific patients/symptoms (even hypothetically), posts that promise specific cures.

### 6. Reviews — STI-specific cultivation

**Default review request:** doesn't mention STI at all. "Could you leave us a Google review about your visit?" — most patients won't mention STI even when they came for it.

**Targeted STI review request** (use sparingly, ~1 per 10 patients): when a patient who came specifically for an STI test has a good experience, you CAN ask them to mention the testing in their review — phrased as their option, not your script.

- ✅ "If you're comfortable, mentioning that you came for testing helps other patients find us — but only if you'd like to share that."
- ❌ "Please mention STD testing in your review."

The prior audit found only **3.2% of reviews network-wide mention STI/STD/HIV keywords**. A 5–10% mention rate is achievable and signals to Google's matching that the clinic is relevant for STI searches. Don't engineer beyond that — Google's review-spam detection is sensitive to topic clustering.

### 7. Q&A — STI questions seeded at launch

Pre-populate these in the profile's Q&A within Day 1–7:

1. "Is the STD testing confidential? Do you share results with anyone?" → Yes, fully confidential; results go only to the patient.
2. "Can I walk in for an STI test or do I need to book?" → Both available; booking recommended for faster service.
3. "How much does an STD panel cost?" → Range, transparent. Address the pricing-perception issue we see in reviews.
4. "How long do test results take?" → Real timeline.
5. "Do you treat HIV?" → Yes, treatment and ongoing management.
6. "Can I bring my partner for STI testing?" → Yes, partner-notification support is included.
7. "What's the window period for an HIV test after exposure?" → Real medical answer.
8. "What's the difference between an STD and STI?" → Brief clinical explainer.

Pre-seeding Q&A is a legitimate GBP feature — Google allows the business to ask AND answer common questions. Don't fake patient questions, but DO post your own and answer them.

Add these to each of the Day 30 / 60 / 90 / quarterly checks:

| STI services in list | 30 (full template) | <25 |
| STD clinic / STD testing service / HIV testing center categories present | All 3 | Any missing |

| STI-themed posts last 90d | 3 (one per month) | 0 |

## When to STOP applying this playbook and look elsewhere

If a clinic has:
- All 30 STI services listed ✅
- STD/STI testing in the title ✅
- All 3 STI categories present ✅
- 4–6% STI keyword impressions (matches network) ✅
- 3–8% STI-mentioning reviews ✅
- 100% reply rate on STI-mentioning reviews ✅

…**and still has <12% STI consultation tagging** (Allo's low-tier definition), then the GMB playbook is not the lever. Per the prior audit, escalate to:

1. **EMR diagnosis-tagging audit** at that clinic. Are STI patients being recorded under `ed_plus` / `pe_plus` / `others` instead of `sti`?
2. **In-clinic intake review.** When patients call/walk in mentioning STI symptoms, do they get steered to consultations that get tagged STI? Or routed to a general "men's sexual health" appointment that gets tagged something else?
3. **Paired patient comparison** — pick 10 patients at the low-STI clinic and 10 at a top-STI clinic (e.g. Velachery) with the same presenting complaint. Compare what tag_type each got.

The known clinics to apply this escalation to (from the prior audit): Garkheda Aurangabad · Saraswathipuram Mysuru · Ashok Nagar Ranchi · Kalyan West Mumbai · Tatya Tope Nagar Nagpur.
